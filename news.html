    SIGNING OF USUFRUCT DEED BETWEEN LGU-BUHI AND CSPC

    MICHAEL C. COLINA

    To invest high-quality education, a conducive learning environment is necessary. The 1987 Philippine Constitution adopts as a matter of principle under Section 17 thereof, that the State shall give priority to education and various other allied fields as potent means to foster patriotism and nationalism, accelerate social progress, and promote total human liberation and development. Republic Act No. 7722 or the Higher Education Act of 1994 provides for a broad agenda for reforms in the country’s education system. The law established the Higher Education Institutions (HEIs) both public and private, which have demonstrated the highest degree or level of standards along with the areas of instruction, research, and extension of their particular fields or courses, CSPC included. In reciprocation to the policies of the Local Government Unit of Buhi and the CSPC, both have come to terms as regards to the opening of CSPC Buhi Campus facilities to cater to the growing population of tertiary level students especially from the marginalized sector. The present site of CSPC campus at San Buenaventura has ostensibly become inadequate in terms of space to accommodate the enrollees. The Local Government Unit of Buhi through Expropriation Ordinance No. 13-130, Series of 2013 and Resolution No. 13-016 dated February 4, 2013, has initiated expropriation proceedings to cultivate a parcel of the lot located at Lourdes, Buhi, Camarines Sur for public use under an Order from the Honorable Court, the subject property has already been delivered to the possession of the LGU and therefore has already the rightful claim or prerogative over the same. As a result of the technical conference to

    resolve the issues about the immediate establishment of the CSPC Buhi Campus, there arises the need for the Lawful Possessor to extend the authority and legal personality for the Usufructuary over the property delivered to the possession of the former. The term of the usufruct shall be for fifty (50) years. The lot area to be occupied and ownership has a total of 10,000 square meters will be allowed for the use of the Usufructuary as the locale of the CSPC Buhi Campus. In docility with the overall Site Development Plan and other lawful requirements, the Usufructuary shall introduce improvements and equipment which are imperative for the operation of the CSPC Buhi Campus.  This deed of usufruct made and executed by and among the Municipal Government of Buhi, Camarines Sur, represented by its Municipal Mayor, Hon. Margarita M. Aguinillo; hereinafter referred to as the Lawful Possessor; and the Camarines Sur Polytechnic Colleges (CSPC for brevity), represented in this exemplar by its College President, Dr. Charlito P. Cadag, referred to as the Usufructuary and DepEd representative Dr. Loida N. Nidea, School Principal. Signed in the presence of Municipal Vice Mayor - Jose Alfred N. Balagot, Municipal Councilor, Chairperson SB Committee on Legal Matters and Ordinances - Loreto M. Camasis, Vice President of Academic Affairs - Maria Laarni M. Salcedo, Ph.D., and Vice President for Administration and Finance - Ferdinand B. Valencia. This consortium was signed last January 20, 2020, held at Sangguniang Bayan (SB), Session Hall.

    NSTP-CWTS Students of Buhi Campus at its best in serving Buhi Community

    JOANA ROSEM. RAPIÑAN, JEANYLORD PAJENAGO AND DIONEZA ESPIRITU

    The National Service Training Program (NSTP) is a Civic Education and defense preparedness program for student instituted by the government on January 5, 2000 by virtue of Republic Act 9163 or commonly known as National Service Training Program (NSTP) Act of 2001. There are three components of NSTP namely; Reserve Officers Training Corps (ROTC), Civic Welfare Training Service (CWTS) and Literacy Training Service (LTS). ROTC provides military training in order to motivate, train, organize and mobilize the youth for national defense preparedness. CWTS refers to the activities contributory for the general welfare and betterment of life for the members of the community. LTS trains students to teach literacy and numerical skills to the children, out–of-school youth and other member of the society in need for their help. Last September 29, 2019, NSTP-CWTS Buhi Campus participated in the NSTP day held at CSPC Nabua Campus wherein each NSTP components contends with each other by dint of games, pageantry, battle of the brain and many more. The said event served as an opportunity to help students be socially attached to one another. Many activities were facilitated by the NSTP coordinators and other professors to make sure that their students are well-behaved and to give assistance if needed. Such activities brought out the natural competitiveness of the students and helped them develop their social skills. NSTP-CWTS Buhi also trains students on agriculture where students are taught how to plant and farm. They also taught the students how to work hard to earn money by collecting scraps around the poblacion area and helped them improve their skills on entrepreneurships by coming up with their own sales concepts, strategies and many more. As a result of these activities, NSTP-CWTS Buhi at its action to Buhinon through gift giving and feeding programs for many pupils in selected elementary schools in Buhi. Through these activities, the students have built passion not only in one particular course and noticeably improved their overall skills. There were orientation and seminars prepared by the NSTP coordinator of CSPC to help train its students to recognize the youth’s critical role in nation-building, to promote consciousness among other youth and develop their physical, moral, spiritual, intellectual and social well-being. The seminar helps to educate the students of their vital role in the society, be socially aware, trained and spiritually oriented of the purposes of the NSTP components. With these continues seminars, workshops, recreations and activities held by the NSTP coordinators and professors NSTP-CWTS-Buhi has seen a huge improvement on its student in various way. NSTP-CWTS Buhi together with its hardworking and well attached professors has been teaching NSTP-CWTS students at their best. With sincerity, trains them accordingly with passion and care, thus, active participation and well trained student were the results of their hard work and proper training method. CSPC Buhi Campus and staff have been serving and educating its students with great satisfactory and continuously share their wisdom and courage to the students to be successful future professionals not just in our country but internationally.

    COVID-19 PANDEMIC: A Changed World

    JOBERT CANON, JALLEN DACUBA AND PEARL ANN ALAPAD

    The coronavirus disease 2019 (COVID-19) pandemic is the defining global health crisis of the time and the greatest challenge the world is facing nowadays. This pandemic is much more than health crisis as it is also an unprecedented socio-economic crisis. Stressing all of the countries, it touches and has the potential to create devastating health, social, economic and political effects that may leave a longstanding scars. COVID-19 is rapidly spreading around the world. In order to slowdown the transmission of the disease in the community, many of the affected countries have decided to undergo total lockdown. Due to lockdown, major international flights and all types of business transports have been deferred amid different countries. Furthermore, people belonging to the tourism and transportation industry are highly affected and facing utmost difficulties. Also, the economy of the countries are facing the threat of high inflation and increasing of unemployment. Every day, people are losing jobs and income with no way of knowing when normality will return, which could mean thousands or millions of people will not be able to meet their basic needs. Aside from that, the educational sector is also been affected, where some countries have to declared a suspension, while some are making ways to move forward such as online classes. The pandemic also brought changed to the way how the people live, in where, physical distancing is needed, wearing face mask and regularly washing of hands. In additon, this Covid 19 has affected the sources of supply and effects the global economy. There are restrictions of travelling from one country to another country. During travelling numbers of cases are identified positive when tested, especially when they are taking international visits. All governments, health organizations and other authorities are continuously cynosuring on identifying the cases affected by the Covid-19 pandemic. Health care professional face a lot of difficulties in maintaining the quality of healthcare in these days. One of the key challenges in managing this public health crisis has been the production and distribution of medical devices, surgical supplies and pharmaceuticals to those on the clinical frontline. COVID-19 pandemic has undoubtedly put forth remarkably bad effects on the day to day life of the entire human society and on the world economy. However, the disease can only be curtailed by means of social awareness and caution. Every country needs to act promptly to prepare, respond, recover and build resilience to crisis and shocks.

    COLLEGE NURSE VISITED THE BUHI CAMPUS

    JOBERT SJ. CANON

    On 12th day of March, 2020, CSPC College Nurse visited Buhi Campus. Resident Nurse Jerome Francia demonstrated and discussed the causes and effects of the virus, so as to maintain a healthy immune system. Nurse Francia discussed that some of its mild symptoms includes fever, cough and colds, and difficulty of breathing. In severe cases, it may cause to Pneumonia, Acute Respiratory Syndrome, kidney disease and even death. In order to prevent these factors, Nurse Francia taught the students to do social distancing (at least 1 meter away), a regular washing of hands (for at least 20 seconds) and to wear face mask. According to the World Health Organization (WHO) named the controversial virus as COVID-19, NCOV before, is from a family of viruses that cause many illnesses, from a simple cough and cold up to severe infection. It can be foist through inhalation of droplets of saliva from an infected person, and this is called droplet transmission. With its rampancy all around the country, CSPC College Nurse raised awareness and prevention tips aversely on COVID-19 so as to minimize and help the government stop the proliferation of the virus. It is a tough battle that the world is facing at present. But as one world, interdependently and mutually uniting to fight against COVID-19, we will heal as one.
